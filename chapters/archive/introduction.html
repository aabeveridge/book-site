Hope Archive

Providing Access to Data and Methods

The archive and tutorial provide readers with an opportunity to explore the Obama Hope iconography, to use the archived data to address altogether new research questions, or to learn iconographic tracking methods by following the provided tutorial. As this collection takes up experimental methods, we hope our readers will explore (test, play, augment) the methods discussed in this collection, and the archive and tutorial are intended to facilitate readers' explorations. As such, this collection is not meant for reading and viewing alone, and with this pragmatic addition we provide readers with tools to conduct their own research in the future.

The "Tutorial" page, written by Kristina Bowers, provides an introduction to Iconographic Tracking methods, and explains how to collect images, how to code those images according to their genre/circulation, and then how to visualize the results of the coding using Google Sheets or AtlasTi. This tutorail is intended for researchers who are new to digital visual research, and it provides a basic introduction to Iconographic Tracking methods.

Archive

This archive provides access to two datasets. The first is Laurie Gries' collection of Obamicons that have formed the basis for this collection, for many other articles and collection chapters produced by Gries and collaborators, and for Gries own monograph, Still Life with Rhetoric. The images in Gries collection have been archived with archive.org's Wayback Machine, and ensures that the images are saved along with any other network/link metadata. Linkrot and post/page deletions represent real, material problems for digital researchers, and it is imperative to support and utilize tools like the Wayback Machine in preserving open data for research and collaboration.

To access Gries' collection of Obamicons, download the following CSV file below. To do so, right-click or ctrl-click obamicons.csv and save it to your computer. You will then be able to open it in your preferred spreadsheet or analysis software.

Obamicon Archive: obamicons.csv

The second dataset, archiving all of the Twitter posts mentioning Sheppard Fairey's Obama Hope poster from 2008 to 2016, was produced using MassMine. MassMine is a data collection toolset, facilitating the collection of data from Twitter and other digital networks for interdisciplinary research. MassMine is currently funded by the National Endowment for the Humanities, and full user documention, tutorials, and research examples are available at massmine.org. In addition to using MassMine to collect the data, we have used DocNow's Tweet catalog to archive the tweet IDs for the Obama Hope related tweets.

To access the Obama Hope related tweets, please go to the DocNow Catalog page for the tweets: Obama Hope Tweets. You will need to rehydrate the tweets (using the Tweet ID's to collect full tweet data from Twitter's API) by using MassMine's twitter-rehydrate functionality. DocNow also provides a rehydrate tool on their website as well. For additional tutorials please see MassMine's Twitter Analysis page.
